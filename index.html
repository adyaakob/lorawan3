Executive Summary

Key Features

  • Real-time tracking of 4,500 workers across nine estates
  • LoRaWAN technology for reliable coverage
  • Solar-powered infrastructure
  • Integration with existing HQ systems

This technical proposal outlines a comprehensive solution for real-time tracking of workers across nine oil palm estates, each housing approximately 500 workers. The system leverages LoRaWAN technology to overcome the challenges of limited GPS and cellular coverage in dense plantation environments.

Project Background

In modern plantation operations, ensuring worker safety and optimizing operational efficiency are paramount concerns. Traditional methods of worker tracking and management often fall short in providing real-time information and quick response capabilities in emergency situations.

System Purpose

  • Enhance worker safety through real-time location tracking
  • Improve emergency response times and effectiveness
  • Optimize workforce management and operational efficiency
  • Provide data-driven insights for better decision making

Implementation Scope

  • Coverage area: Nine oil palm estates
  • Total workers: 4,500 (approximately 500 per estate)
  • Infrastructure: LoRaWAN gateways with solar power
  • Integration points: HQ management systems

Technology Selection

LoRaWAN technology was chosen for its:

  • Long-range coverage (up to 10km in rural areas)
  • Low power consumption for extended battery life
  • Ability to penetrate dense foliage
  • Cost-effective deployment and maintenance

Expected Benefits

  • Enhanced worker safety through real-time monitoring
  • Improved emergency response capabilities
  • Optimized workforce management
  • Reduced operational costs
  • Better compliance with safety regulations

Proposed Solution

Background

Oil palm plantations face unique challenges in worker management and safety due to their vast, densely vegetated areas. Traditional GPS and cellular-based tracking systems often struggle with signal penetration under the palm canopy.

Objectives

  • Implement reliable real-time worker tracking
  • Overcome GPS and cellular coverage limitations
  • Ensure continuous operation with minimal maintenance
  • Integrate with existing HQ systems

System Architecture

End Device Layer

HKT GT-100 personal trackers worn by workers

Gateway Layer

Milesight SG50 solar-powered LoRaWAN gateways

Application Layer

Cloud-based data management platform and HQ dashboard

Network Topology

Star Topology 1

Multiple HKT GT-100 trackers connect to a single Milesight SG50 gateway

Star Topology 2

Multiple SG50 gateways connect to the central network server

Data Flow

1

Data Collection

HKT GT-100 collects GPS data and sensor information

2

LoRaWAN Transmission

Data transmitted via LoRaWAN to Milesight SG50 Gateway

3

Backhaul Transmission

Gateway sends aggregated data via 4G LTE to Network Server

4

Processing & Storage

Data processed, analyzed, and stored in the cloud

Gateway Infrastructure

The Milesight SG500 8-channel gateway serves as the backbone of our LoRaWAN network, providing reliable connectivity across the plantation.

Gateway Features

  • 8-channel parallel processing
  • Up to 15km range in line of sight
  • 4G/LTE backhaul connectivity
  • GPS time synchronization
  • IP65 weatherproof enclosure

Solar Power System

  • 30W standard solar panel (upgradable to 45W)
  • 25000mAh built-in rechargeable battery
  • 0.8W typical power consumption
  • Up to 4 days operation without sunlight
Smart Power Management
  • Data package filtering to reduce unnecessary power usage
  • Smart Wi-Fi control for battery efficiency
  • Low-temperature battery heating for cold environments

Risk Assessment and Mitigation Strategies

Hardware Failures

Risk of tracker device or gateway hardware malfunctions in harsh plantation environments.

Mitigation Strategies:

  • IP67-rated waterproof and dustproof enclosures
  • Regular maintenance schedules
  • Redundant gateway deployment
  • Backup power systems

Network Coverage

Potential signal interference or coverage gaps in dense plantation areas.

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Comprehensive site survey before deployment
  • Strategic gateway placement
  • Signal strength monitoring
  • Coverage optimization through network planning

Battery Life

Risk of device power failure during critical operations.

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Solar-powered charging systems
  • Low-power optimization
  • Battery level monitoring
  • Preventive battery replacement schedule
